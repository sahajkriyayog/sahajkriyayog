### Introduction and Lineage
The text is a spiritual booklet published by the Gita Prachar Mandali of the Brahmarshi Satyananda Sannyas Ashram. It was written to introduce the general public to the profound science of Kriya Yoga. The author notes that just as one cannot truly know the taste of a sweet without eating it, one must practice Kriya Yoga to genuinely understand it. 

The text lists a distinguished lineage of Gurus who propagated this science, beginning with **Bhagavan Sri Sri Babaji Maharaj**, followed by his direct disciple **Sri Sri Lahiri Baba (Shyamacharan Lahiri Mahasaya)**, and extending through prominent masters like Swami Sriyukteswar Giri, Swami Yogananda Giri, Swami Satyananda Giri, and several others down to Swami Jnanananda Giri.

### What is Yoga?
The word "Yoga" originates from the Sanskrit root *"Yuj"*, meaning to join or connect. According to Sage Patanjali, yoga is the cessation of the modifications of the mind. At its core, yoga is the union of the individual soul (Jivatma) with the Supreme Soul (Paramatma). Various scriptures, including the Bhagavad Gita and Shiva Samhita, emphasize that without the practice of yoga, true liberation and self-realization are impossible, even for the gods. There are four primary types of yoga: Hatha, Raja, Mantra, and Laya, which do not contradict each other but rather serve as complementary paths. 

### The Origins of Kriya Yoga
In 1861, in a Himalayan cave, the immortal yogi **Babaji Maharaj initiated Lahiri Mahasaya into the ancient science of Kriya Yoga (Raja Yoga)**. Babaji revealed that this was the exact same science Lord Krishna had imparted to Arjuna millennia ago, which had been lost over time due to human indifference. 

Lahiri Mahasaya was a householder, and he was deeply moved when Babaji restored his memories of past lives. Babaji instructed him to remain a householder so that ordinary people lost in worldly illusions could have an accessible path to God-realization. By synthesizing the four major yogas, Babaji created the modern system of Kriya Yoga. 

### The Three Pillars of Kriya Yoga Practice
According to Patanjali, Kriya Yoga consists of three essential components, which Lahiri Mahasaya simplified for modern practitioners:
*   **Tapasya (Austerity):** Physical discipline, such as dietary control, minimal sleep, and yoga postures, designed to purify the body and calm the senses. 
*   **Svadhyaya (Self-study):** Verbal austerity, which involves studying scriptures, speaking truthfully, and avoiding harmful or unnecessary talk to bring focus inward.
*   **Ishvara Pranidhana (Devotion):** Mental surrender and dedicating the fruits of all actions to God.

**Swami Sriyukteswar Giri noted that Kriya Yoga rapidly accelerates human evolution; just half a minute of Kriya practice equals one year of natural spiritual growth**. The practice brings the life force (Prana) inward. While a normal human breathes roughly 18 times a minute, Kriya Yoga drastically slows this rate down, leading to profound inner peace and an awakening of the subtle nerve centers in the spine. Through this science, a yogi can achieve self-realization in a single lifetime, bypassing a million years of natural evolution.

### The Stages and Techniques
Lahiri Mahasaya organized Kriya Yoga into four progressive stages (First to Fourth Kriya). The First Kriya consists of five primary techniques:
1.  **Mahamudra:** Removes physical blockages, keeps the body healthy, and calms mental restlessness.
2.  **Nabhimudra:** Balances the fiery energy in the navel center, greatly aiding mental concentration.
3.  **Pranayama:** Deep, conscious breathing that expands lung capacity, highly oxygenates the blood, and stills the breath by drawing the life force inward.
4.  **Brahma Yoni Mudra:** Enables the practitioner to hear the divine cosmic sound (Anahata/Om) and perceive the divine light.
5.  **Khechari Mudra:** Often beginning with 'Talavya Kriya', this involves moving in the "inner space" and is likened to riding the divine chariot of Arjuna.

### The Supreme Guru: Babaji Maharaj
**Babaji Maharaj is a supremely liberated (*paramukta*) yogi who has retained his physical form in the Himalayas for approximately 600 years**. He takes various forms at will and can only be seen if he wishes it. In the past, he initiated great masters like Adi Shankaracharya and Kabir Das. Swami Sriyukteswar Giri, who met Babaji multiple times, described him as having a youthful, golden complexion with deep, dark eyes, looking identical to a younger Lahiri Mahasaya. 

Babaji taught by example. Once, Lahiri Mahasaya found Babaji washing the dirty brass pot of an ordinary, hashish-smoking sadhu. When Lahiri Mahasaya asked why he was doing this, Babaji smiled, teaching him the ultimate lesson of humility and equal vision.
