🕉️ Foundation

What is Yoga?

The word "Yoga" originates from the Sanskrit root "Yuj", meaning to join or connect. According to Sage Patanjali, yoga is the cessation of the modifications of the mind.

At its core, yoga is the union of the individual soul (Jivatma) with the Supreme Soul (Paramatma). Various scriptures, including the Bhagavad Gita and Shiva Samhita, emphasize that without the practice of yoga, true liberation and self-realization are impossible, even for the gods.

✨ Origins

The Origins of Kriya Yoga

In 1861, in a Himalayan cave, the immortal yogi Babaji Maharaj initiated Lahiri Mahasaya into the ancient science of Kriya Yoga (Raja Yoga).

Babaji revealed that this was the exact same science Lord Krishna had imparted to Arjuna millennia ago, which had been lost over time due to human indifference.

Lahiri Mahasaya was a householder, and Babaji instructed him to remain so, so that ordinary people lost in worldly illusions could have an accessible path to God-realization. By synthesizing the four major yogas, Babaji created the modern system of Kriya Yoga.

🔱 The Practice

The Three Pillars of Kriya Yoga

According to Patanjali, Kriya Yoga consists of three essential components, simplified by Lahiri Mahasaya for modern practitioners.

🔥

Tapasya

Austerity — Physical Discipline

Physical discipline, such as dietary control, minimal sleep, and yoga postures, designed to purify the body and calm the senses. Through tapasya, the practitioner builds the foundation of bodily and mental endurance required for deeper practices.

📖

Svadhyaya

Self-study — Verbal Austerity

Verbal austerity, which involves studying scriptures, speaking truthfully, and avoiding harmful or unnecessary talk to bring focus inward. Svadhyaya refines the mind and sharpens the intellect for spiritual discernment.

🙏

Ishvara Pranidhana

Devotion — Mental Surrender

Mental surrender and dedicating the fruits of all actions to God. Through complete devotion and surrender, the practitioner transcends the ego and opens to divine grace.

The Five Techniques of First Kriya

Lahiri Mahasaya organized Kriya Yoga into four progressive stages. The First Kriya consists of these five primary techniques.

Mahamudra removes physical blockages, keeps the body healthy, and calms mental restlessness. This foundational technique prepares the body for deeper meditation by clearing the energy channels and establishing physical equilibrium. It is the gateway through which the practitioner enters the inner realm of Kriya Yoga practice.

Nabhimudra balances the fiery energy in the navel center, greatly aiding mental concentration. The navel is considered the seat of manipura chakra, the center of willpower and transformation. By mastering this mudra, the practitioner gains control over the digestive fire and channels the vital energy upward for higher spiritual purposes.
